# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy
*Getting the first 100 users for $0*

## The Target Persona
Our ideal early adopter is a **Series A or Series B Startup CTO / VP of Engineering**. 
They manage teams of 20–100 engineers and have largely decentralized their software purchasing. Their developers are swiping corporate cards for Cursor, ChatGPT, and Claude independently. These leaders know they are overspending on duplicate AI tools, but they lack the time and centralized visibility to manually audit every credit card statement. 
**What they Google or scroll right before needing this:**
Right before they want this, they are staring at an AWS or Brex corporate card statement, Googling things like *"How to track SaaS spend across departments,"* *"GitHub Copilot vs Cursor ROI,"* or they are scrolling through a thread on r/SaaS about *"How to cut engineering burn rate before Series B."*

**The Unfair Distribution Channel:**
Our unfair advantage is the **"Shareable Audit" viral loop built directly into the product**. Competitors rely on SEO. We rely on ego. Engineering leaders love proving they run a tight ship. By giving them a beautifully formatted URL that says "My team's AI spend is perfectly optimized," they will naturally share it in Slack channels and on Twitter, functioning as free billboards for Credex to other CTOs.

## The Core Value Proposition
**"Find out exactly how much your engineers are wasting on duplicate AI subscriptions in 30 seconds."** 

We are not selling a SaaS product; we are giving away a high-value calculator. SubTract functions as a "wedge" product. It provides immediate, undeniable, personalized financial value (a custom audit) for free, with zero setup required, acting as a lead magnet for the core Credex offering.

## $0 Acquisition Channels (The First 100 Users)

### 1. Engineering Leadership Communities (Slack / Discord)
I will infiltrate private engineering leadership communities like *Rands Leadership Slack* and *CTO Craft*. 
**The tactic:** Avoid direct promotion. Instead, post the interactive tool as a resource. 
*Draft message:* "Hey all, my team realized we were bleeding cash on ghost seats for GitHub Copilot and ChatGPT because devs were buying them independently. I built a quick React tool to calculate exactly how much money a team is wasting on overlapping AI tools based on team size. Thought it might be useful for anyone else doing Q3 budget planning."

### 2. "Roast My Stack" on HackerNews / Twitter
Developers love optimizing things, and they love showing off.
**The tactic:** Launch on HackerNews (Show HN) and Twitter with a contrarian take.
*Draft message:* "Show HN: SubTract. 80% of startups are paying for AI tools their devs aren't using. Put in your tech stack, and my engine will aggressively roast your AI spend and tell you what to cut."
By adding the "Shareable URL" feature to the product, every user who completes an audit is incentivized to share their "score" on Twitter, creating a viral loop.

### 3. Direct Cold Email to Target Startups
Using Crunchbase, I will filter for companies that recently raised a Series A (they have cash but are beginning to hire CFOs who care about burn rate). 
**The tactic:** Send a 2-sentence email to the CTO.
*Draft message:* "Hey [Name], I noticed [Company] scaled the engineering team recently. We built a free tool that scans your AI vendor stack (Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT) and identifies exactly where you have duplicate seats or overspend. Takes 30 seconds. Worth a look before your next budget review?"

## Measuring Week 1 Traction
I will measure success not by website visits, but by the **Lead Conversion Rate**.
If 1,000 people visit the site and do an audit, but 0 people enter their email to "Partner with Credex", the GTM strategy failed because the wedge didn't convert. 
**Target:** 5% of completed audits (where savings > $500) convert into a qualified email lead.
